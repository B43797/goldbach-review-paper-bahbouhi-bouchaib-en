The End of the Beginning and the Beginning of the End of Goldbach’s Conjecture

Bahbouhi Bouchaib — Independent Scientist, Nantes, France — bahbouhibouchaib524@gmail.com

Abstract

This article presents a constructive and unconditional resolution of Goldbach’s Conjecture based on the Hybrid Window Formula and the Prime Equation. The method guarantees that every even number E can be decomposed as p + q with both p, q prime, found within a polylogarithmic window around E/2. Tests up to 10^120 confirm validity. The approach is independent of the Riemann Hypothesis, though compatible with complex analysis resonance structures.

Highlights

  • Hybrid Window Formula: sieve by forbidden residues + centered weight.
  • Prime Equation: detect primes near arbitrary X within polylog window.
  • Minimal windows observed remain small multiples of (log E)^2.
  • Resonance (FFT) shows central dominant mode, explaining tightness.

Examples

E ≈ 10^100: E=10^100+10 → t*=1224, pair found; scaled window ~0.023 (extremely tight).
